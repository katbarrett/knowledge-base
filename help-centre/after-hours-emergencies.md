# After-Hours Emergencies

We are not open 24 hours, but we want to make sure your pet gets help when they need it most. If your pet is experiencing an emergency outside of clinic hours, please contact one of the emergency hospitals below.

## Emergency Clinics in Toronto

**Toronto Veterinary Emergency Hospital (TVEH)**
21 Rolark Drive, Scarborough
(416) 247-8387
Open 24 hours

**VEC — Veterinary Emergency Clinic**
920 Yonge Street, Toronto
(416) 920-2002
Open 24 hours

## Signs That Require Immediate Emergency Care

Do not wait for a regular appointment if your pet is experiencing any of the following:

- Difficulty breathing or choking
- Collapse or inability to stand
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Suspected poisoning or toxin ingestion
- Seizures
- Suspected broken bones
- Bloated or distended abdomen (especially in large-breed dogs)
- Loss of consciousness
- Eye injuries
- Inability to urinate (especially in cats — this is a life-threatening emergency)

## When in Doubt

If you are unsure whether your pet's condition is an emergency, call one of the emergency clinics above. Their teams can help you assess the situation over the phone.

## Returning to Us After an Emergency

If your pet is seen at an emergency clinic, please let us know so we can follow up on their care and update their records. You can email us at hello@pawsclinic.com or call us the next business day.
