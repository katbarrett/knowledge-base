# What to Bring to Your Appointment

A little preparation goes a long way toward making your visit smooth and productive.

## All Visits

- Your pet in a secure carrier (cats and small animals) or on a leash (dogs)
- Any previous medical records if you are a new patient
- A list of any medications or supplements your pet is currently taking
- Your health insurance details if applicable

## Wellness and Vaccination Visits

- Previous vaccination records if available
- A fresh stool sample (collected within 24 hours) if a fecal test has been requested

## Sick Visits

- Notes on your pet's symptoms — when they started, how they've changed, and anything that seems to trigger them
- A urine sample if your pet has been having urinary symptoms (collected in a clean container within 2 hours of the appointment)

## Surgery or Procedure Visits

- Confirmation that your pet has fasted as instructed (we will confirm fasting requirements when you book)
- Emergency contact information in case we need to reach you during the procedure

## Tips for a Stress-Free Visit

Bring a favourite toy or blanket with a familiar scent to help keep your pet calm. Avoid feeding your pet immediately before travel if they are prone to motion sickness.
